Car GST Reduction in India


1. New GST Reform (GST 2.0) Implemented The Indian government reformed the GST structure for automobiles, effective 22 September 2025, to simplify and reduce taxes on car purchases. Business Standard.

2. Lower GST on Small Cars Small petrol, LPG, or CNG cars (engine ≤ 1200 cc and length ≤ 4000 mm) and similar diesel cars (engine ≤ 1500 cc) now attract 18% GST, down from around 28% plus additional cess earlier. Animal Husbandry and Dairying

3. Flat 40% GST for Larger Cars and SUVs Mid-size, large cars, SUVs, MPVs, and many utility vehicles now come under 40% GST without the earlier compensation cess. This means overall tax burden has reduced even if the slab seems higher than 28%. Animal Husbandry and Dairying

4. Luxury Cars Also Benefit High-end and luxury cars previously faced GST + cess totalling up to roughly 45–50%. Under the new system, they are taxed at a flat 40% GST, making them cheaper by several lakhs for customers. Business Standard

5. Removal of Compensation Cess The compensation cess (which added up to ~22% tax on many vehicles) has been abolished, reducing the overall tax burden and helping lower on-road car prices. Business Standard

6. Car Makers Passing on Savings Major automakers such as Maruti Suzuki, Tata Motors, Mahindra, Volkswagen, BMW and others have reduced ex-showroom car prices to pass on the GST benefits to buyers. The Times of India +2 Business Today +2

7. Example of Price Reductions Maruti Suzuki cut prices by up to around ₹1.29 lakh on models like S-Presso, Baleno, and Brezza. The Times of India Mahindra reduced SUV prices by over ₹1.5 lakh on models like XUV3XO. Business Today BMW cut luxury car prices by up to around ₹9–10 lakh post-GST changes. 1tak

8. Overall Impact on Buyers Buyers of small and mid-range cars now pay less tax, lowering the ex-showroom and on-road prices. Larger cars and luxury vehicles also see net savings due to cess removal and new 40% slab. This reform encourages higher car sales, especially in the festive season.
